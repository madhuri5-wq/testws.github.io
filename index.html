About

Background & Expertise

Dr. Arjun Mehta is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Metallurgical and Materials Engineering at IIT Kharagpur, one of India's premier institutes of technology. His research lies at the intersection of computational materials science and experimental mechanics, with a particular focus on understanding deformation behaviour and phase transformations in advanced structural alloys.

Before joining IIT Kharagpur in 2021, Dr. Mehta was a postdoctoral researcher at the Max-Planck-Institut für Eisenforschung, Düsseldorf, where he worked on machine-learning-guided alloy design. He completed his doctoral degree from IISc Bangalore and his undergraduate studies from NIT Trichy.

His work is supported by funding from DST-SERB, BRNS, and industry collaborations with Tata Steel and SAIL. He is a recipient of the DST-INSPIRE Faculty Award and the Materials Research Society of India Young Scientist Award.

Research

Areas of Focus

High-Entropy Alloys

Developing multi-principal element alloys with exceptional mechanical properties for extreme-environment applications in aerospace and nuclear sectors.

HEA Multi-principal Refractory

Phase-Field Modelling

Using phase-field and CALPHAD-integrated simulations to predict microstructural evolution during solidification and solid-state phase transformations.

CALPHAD MOOSE DFT

ML-Guided Alloy Design

Applying machine learning and data-driven approaches to accelerate the discovery of novel alloy compositions with targeted property profiles.

Gaussian Process Bayesian Opt. ICME

Deformation Mechanics

Understanding the fundamental mechanisms of plastic deformation — twinning, dislocation dynamics, and slip-twin interactions — using EBSD and TEM.

EBSD TEM In-situ XRD

Additive Manufacturing

Exploring process–structure–property relationships in additively manufactured metallic components, particularly residual stress evolution and defect formation.

L-PBF DED Synchrotron

Corrosion & Oxidation

Investigating high-temperature oxidation kinetics and aqueous corrosion behaviour of structural alloys for energy and chemical process industries.

EIS TGA Thermogravimetry
